Semester GPA / SGPA Formula

SGPA = Σ (Course Grade Point × Course Credit Hours) ÷ Σ Course Credit Hours

Example: Three courses have grade points and credits of 4.00 × 3, 3.50 × 3, and 3.00 × 2. Quality points = 12 + 10.5 + 6 = 28.5. Total credits = 8. SGPA = 28.5 ÷ 8 = 3.56.

Cumulative GPA / CGPA Formula

CGPA = Σ (Semester SGPA × Semester Credit Hours) ÷ Σ Semester Credit Hours

Example: Semester 1 has 3.40 SGPA over 15 credits and Semester 2 has 3.70 SGPA over 18 credits. Quality points = 51 + 66.6 = 117.6. Total credits = 33. CGPA = 117.6 ÷ 33 = 3.56.

Complete Grading-System Regulations

  1. Grading of the students shall be made as per the table above.
  2. Maximum possible Grade Point Average is 4.00.
  3. The fraction of marks obtained in a course shall be counted as one mark, e.g. 60.3 shall be considered as 60 while 59.5 or more is to be considered as 60.
  4. Grade Point Average (GPA) is an expression for the average performance of the student in the courses he has taken during any semester; thus GPA may be calculated for 1st semester, 2nd, or any other semester.
  5. CGPA and GPA shall be rounded to 2 decimal places, e.g. a CGPA/GPA of 3.084285 shall be reported as 3.08, while a CGPA/GPA of 3.065124 shall be reported as 3.07.
  6. If a student fails a course he shall be awarded “F” for failure in a given course grade, representing that he has failed the course in that semester. He shall repeat the failed course whenever offered again. In case he passes the same in the subsequent semester it shall be signified by “R/Repeat,” showing that it is a repeated course passed later on.
  7. It shall be mandatory for a student to pass both written and viva of a research project/research thesis/research report at all levels where the research project has been opted for degree completion.
